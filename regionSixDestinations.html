Top 5

Bulabog Beach

Bulabog Beach - This is the 2.5km long water sports beach of Boracay, and is located on the east side of the island. From November to April, Bulabog Beach is the kite boarding and windsurfing heart of Boracay. From May to October, it is the water sports centre of Boracay. It is a 5- minute walk to D’Mall, 10 minutes to White Beach.

There are some fantastic resorts and hotels that take advantage of this unique beach community. The beach is protected by an offshore reef.

It is less developed and the Boracay hotels here are usually quieter than those on White Beach. Bulabog has earned the title: “Number One kite boarding beach in Asia.” See more in Bulabog Beach



Top 4

Alona Beach

Alona Beach - Situated in Panglao Island is a narrow strip of white sand beach that is can be considered as one of the best diving destinations in the world, the Alona Beach. It earned its fame for snorkeling and diving because it is located near a house reef, where corals and colorful fishes can be seen without the need for any equipment.

Its shore stretches 1.5 kilometers long and is walkable from end to end in 10-15 minutes. Amusingly, its name is derived from a local actress named Alona Alegre, who shot a film at the beach during the 70s when the beach was still vacant and was so beloved by the locals for her politeness that they named the beach after her.

How to get there

Travelers can get to Alona Beach from Bohol's capital city, Tagbilaran, which is about 22 KM away. From the city, you can take a car and cross either the Dauis Bridge or Borja Bridge to get to Panglao Island. The beach sits in the southwest part of the island. Meanwhile, guests who arrive at the Panglao International Airport can also take a car or tricycle directly to the beach, which is only around 3 KM away. Otherwise, you can join tour packages that include Alona Beach in its itinerary for a more convenient trip.

Best time to visit

Travelers can visit Alona Beach all year round. The best time to visit is around November to April when the weather in the Philippines is cooler. There are also fewer chances of rain during these months, so you can make the most of your trip to this pristine beach. See more in Alona Beach



Top 3

Diniwid Beach

Diniwid Beach - Diniwid Beach is a tranquil destination just a few minutes away from the popular Station 1 area. It is a great spot for anyone wanting to escape the usual white sand beaches of Boracay and is looking for a peaceful and serene ambiance.

It also has various villas or beachfront places for guests who would like to stay in their area and not in Boracay’s beach. Some villas also offer a panoramic view of the beach. As for the restaurants by the beach, most of them serve both local and international cuisines for their guests. Due to its peaceful atmosphere, no loud or busy activities are provided in the area. Instead, more subtle and quiet activities such as swimming and snorkeling are favored on this beach.

How to get there

Diniwid Beach is located a few minutes’ walk from Boracay Station 1.If you’re coming from D’Mall, you can take a car or tricycle to the beach, which is about 3.6 KM away.

Best time to visit

Travelers can visit Diniwid Beach all year round. The best time to go here is from November to April when the weather is cooler and there are fewer chances of rain. See more in Diniwid Beach



Top 2

Yapak Beach (Puka Shell Beach)

Yapak Beach (Puka Shell Beach) - Puka Beach, also known as Yapak Beach, is less touristy and has a peaceful seaside atmosphere. It was once famous for its shiny puka shells, beautiful little shells that can be picked up on the beach after the tide goes out.

Singer Liang Jingru's wedding photos were taken here, and now couples propose marriage here from time to time. Due to its remote location, access to Puka Beach is also inconvenient, but it also creates a beautiful, quiet and private environment.

When you climb over the hills and come here, you can stretch your body and mind and walk with the sea breeze, as if the entire coastline belongs to you, which is extraordinarily wonderful. However, the sand at Puka Beach is rougher than the powdery fine sand of White Beach, and the slope here is not very gentle.

Shells at Puka Beach were a popular accessory material in the 1970s and 1980s, but not every season is now available. During the typhoon season, the sand from the sea will flow back to the beach, covering the scattered shells.

Come here in the evening, quietly looking for shells and watching the sunset, also don't have a lot of fun. It doesn't matter if you can't pick up a shell, there are small vendors selling shell products on the beach, and there are small bracelets and other accessories made by local residents that can be purchased.

Top 1

White Beach boracay

White Beach boracay - White Beach is essentially Boracay Island. As the island's main attraction, this Boracay beach is one of the best Visayas tourist spots and top tourist spots in the Philippines.

Situated in the province of Aklan, White Beach is one of the best beaches in the Philippines and has always been the star of the show in Boracay Island. Travelers from all over the Philippines and the world can’t help but admire this Boracay beach's breathtaking beauty. Boracay Island has earned global recognition for its unique and natural charm. It’s been often cited as one of the world’s most beautiful islands because of its many tourist spots and activities that give thousands of vacationers and couples on their honeymoon in the Philippines countless memorable experiences throughout the year.

From waking up to the sound of the crashing waves to going out for long walks by the Boracay beach every morning, to sitting on the white sand beaches all day to enjoy the idyllic ocean view and swimming in the pristine blue water – there’s so much to satisfy one’s wanderlust craving in this tropical island destination.

After its rehabilitation by the government, Boracay's White Beach is back with a beautiful make-over. The beach is more stunning and captivating than before. There’s a lot more space to bask under the sun and stroll while being serenaded by the sound of the ocean. Despite being one of the country’s most visited tourist destinations, this Boracay beach never loses its charisma. There’s something about its long stretch of white beach and its unbeatable, magical sunsets that makes you go back over and over again.

Best Time to Go to White Beach in Boracay

The weather in Boracay Island is beautiful for most of the year. You can visit the island whenever your heart desires. However, it’s still better to consider factors such as high and low seasons so you can make the most out of your trip. See more in White Beach boracay
