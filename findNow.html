Top 10 Beaches in the Philippines

In the Philippines, a nation of more than 7,000 islands, there are numerous scenic beaches. These breathtaking beaches may be found around the country. Some places are well-known for their fine sand and crystal-clear water, while others are popular for their exciting nightlife.

Among our top favorite beaches, there are probably a few that you may have never heard. This list of the top beaches in the Philippines may inspire some fresh travel plans if you have a thirst for adventure. The distance between each beach may seem far, but the trip is definitely worth the effort!

Top 10: Northern Samar,

Biri Island

- Biri Island in Northern Samar has spectacular rock formations shaped through the years by the raging waves and stormy winds. There are about 7 rock formations on the island, which you can visit depending on the tide. Most can be easily reached at low tide. You'll be navigating sharp and slippery stones though, so it's best to wear sturdy and non-slip footwear. Besides the unique rock formations and pristine waters in Biri Island, you'll also find natural saltwater pools where you can take a dip to refresh.


Top 9: Albay,

Buhatan Beach

- Buhatan Beach in Albay is home to a Spanish Galleon
wreck site, which lies 9 metres below the ocean. There
are plenty of tour companies offering snorkelling and
diving excursions, though it's best if you have diving
experience if you're looking to visit this wreck site. The
beach itself is popular for sunbathing, swimming,
and water sports.


Top 8: Panglao,

Alona Beach

- It is renowned for its fine-grained sand beach, crystal-clear waters, and top-notch diving. The shoreline is lined with bars, restaurants, and spots for outdoor massages; this is where the activity is. Alona Beach is a great location for parties and people-watching. You can go diving or snorkeling during the day. When food stands and live music are present on the shore in the evening, Alona comes to life. It's less busy than most beaches in the Philippines, but some areas can be limited due to development.


Top 7: El Nido,

Nacpan &
Calitang Beach

- Nacpan Beach, which stretches over four kilometers, is regarded as one
of the world's most beautiful beaches for a reason. It's a welcome change
from El Nido's busier beaches or interminable island-hopping excursions.
You can find soft sand and crystal-clear blue waves here; occasionally, the
beach is all to yourself. Nacpan Beach doesn't have any upscale eateries,
although there are some little shacks where you can get some regional fare.
You can climb a small hill at the end of the beach to see the twin beaches
of Nacpan and Calitang. To reach there, you can ride tricycle. Be ready
for a bumpy and even muddy ride because a significant amount of the
route is in poor condition.


Top 6:Camarines Norte,

Calaguas

- A 2-hour boat voyage from the mainland separates the province of Camarines Norte's Calaguas group of islands. This tropical location is difficult to reach, which is one of the reasons it has remained largely unspoiled throughout the years. Tinaga Island is home to its most well-known beach, Mahabang Buhangin (Long Beach), where you may go swimming and camping. Additionally, there are rolling hills and volcanic rocks that overlook the water. If you plan to stay in Calaguas for a day or two, you can rent a nipa hut or cottage. One of its charms is the accommodations' simplicity.


Top 5: Leyte,

Kalanggaman Island

- One of the most stunning islands in the Philippines is
Kalanggaman Island in the province of Leyte. Its long
sandbar that extends from one end of the island is its
main attraction. When the tide recedes and reveals it, it
is pretty spectacular. On the island, camping is permitted
as well. On clear evenings, you may glimpse the
star-studded sky.


Top 4: Siargao,

Daku, Naked & Guyam

- Siargao's Daku, Naked, and Guyam group of islets are reachable by taking an island-hopping trip. The journey to these locations begins at Naked Island, aptly named because it is devoid of any flora or coconut trees. It can get quite hot out there, so be sure to carry sunscreen. Before moving on to the next destination, people enjoy sunbathing on the islet's pristine sand. On one end of Guyam Island, there is a sandy beach, and on the other, there are hard corals. The destination for the last stop is Daku Island, where you may have a lunch of fresh seafood and coconut juice in a rented hut or amidst the palm trees. It's perfect due to its calm, crystal-clear waters and white sand.


Top 3: Caramoan,

Matukad Island

- Off the shore of Caramoan is a little island called Matukad.
Its beaches are made of beautiful, white sand and are bordered
by craggy limestone cliffs. The lagoon, which is home to a lone
enormous milkfish, is its most remarkable feature. You can ask
the boatman for more information about this because it has an
interesting story. Bring your camera, do a little walking to get to
the lagoon, and you might be able to see the milkfish while
you're there. The view is magnificent from above.


Top 2: Camiguin,

White Island

- As an empty sandbar, White Island lacks any trees or man-made structures that would provide shade from the sun. Bring adequate water and sunblock if you plan to go. Snorkeling is enjoyable, but watch out for sea urchins and sea snakes. But the sandbar is incredibly beautiful since the tides and the season affect how big and how small it is. Enjoy the fine sand and clear waters, and take some picture-perfect selfies with the Hibok-Hibok Volcano in the background.


Top 1: Boracay,

White Beach

- About 315 kilometers south of Manila, on the island of Boracay, is where
you will find White Beach. This beach, which is located off the northwest
coast of the Panay Island, is a prime example of the Philippines' and
Borocay's visually stunning natural scenery. There are tropical
surroundings, an expansive ocean vista, and many kinds of fauna and
vegetation. White Beach is nearly three kilometers long and has been
separated into three distinct areas, each with its own special beauty. The
majority of the opulent resorts and hotels are located in Station 1, which
has a calm, low-key atmosphere. Station 2, however, is livelier due to its
sizable shopping, entertainment, and dining districts. The area with the
most bungalows and native homes is Station 3, which is also the
quietest area.
