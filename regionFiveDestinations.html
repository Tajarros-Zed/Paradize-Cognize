Bagasbas Beach

Bagasbas Beach - If you are looking for a surfing spot in the Philippines, head over to Bagasbas Beach in Daet, Camarines Norte. It's a top destination for beginners and pro surfers because of its consistent waves the whole-year-round, making it the country's first surfing spot and the world's 61st.

The mile-long stretch of Bagasbas Beach features sandy bottom and shallow waters which make it safe for surfers. The beach is known for its fine, gray sand with big waves. What I like Bagasbas each, it's quiet and clean. Considering it's a public Beach, the local government was able to maintain the cleanliness of the beach. Aside from surfing, you can enjoy here kiteboarding. This activity combines skills of surfing, snowboarding and wakeboarding with a flying kite.

Bagasbas Beach is considered as one of the best kiteboarding spots in Asia.

The best months to indulge kiteboarding is from November to March Surfing lessons are offered at Bagasbas Aqua Center at P400 per hour which includes board rentals. See more in Bagasbas Beach

Paguriran Island - A little gem of an island is tucked away in a quiet corner of Bacon, Sorsogon – the picturesque Paguriran Island. Paguriran is a tiny group of coral and rock islets located off the white sand beaches of Sawanga in Bacon. It is accessible from the adjacent beach by wading through crystal clear waters, the depth of which may range from ankle to waist depending on the tides. Within the island, coralline and granite crags enclose a shallow lagoon that is perfect for swimming (you have to watch out for sharp corals, though). You see, ‘Paguriran Island’ is really not an island but are assortment of craggy rocks that is shaped like a crater of a volcano. This wonderful formation nestles a lagoon within the sea wherein you can swim around clear aquamarine waters. If you are brave enough and have sturdy hiking sandals with you, trek your way up this islet and you’ll sure be rewarded with an amazing aerial view of Paguriran and the shoreline of Bacon.
